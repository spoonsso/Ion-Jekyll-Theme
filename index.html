Research

Escape Behavior

How does the brain detect incoming objects and determine when to avoid them? I explored the neural mechanisms underlying a visually evoked escape behavior using a combination of detailed behavioral analysis and two-photon calcium imaging of neural activity

  • Learn More

Whole-brain calcium imaging

Recent advances have enabled fast, whole-brain recordings of neural activity at cellular resolution. We used this tool to isolate a statistical generator of spontaneous behavioral patterns.


  • Learn More

Neural Networks

To control behavior, neurons collaborate in circuits that span the entire brain. We synthesized behavioral analysis, whole-brain calcium imaging, and theory to build realistic circuit models that explain the zebrafish optomotor response.

  • Learn More




Biological Data Analysis

We developed a new Harvard course, MCB 112, which communicated modern computational techniques for biology. Topics included expectation-maximization and non-negative matrix factorization.
